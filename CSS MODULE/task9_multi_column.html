Greenfield's Biggest Summer Festival Returns After Five-Year Hiatus

Borough residents rallied together to bring back the beloved annual celebration

By Margaret Sullivan  |  Community Affairs Correspondent

After five long years, the Greenfield Summer Festival is set to return to the heart of our community. Organisers confirmed last Monday that all permits have been secured and preparations are well under way for what promises to be the largest and most vibrant edition of the event yet.

The festival, which first launched in 1993, traditionally draws thousands of visitors from across the borough and surrounding areas. Live music, artisan food stalls, a children's funfair, and a spectacular evening fireworks display are among the confirmed attractions. Local businesses have pledged sponsorship, and the council has allocated additional green space along the riverside to accommodate growing crowds.

"This festival belongs to every resident of Greenfield. We are bringing it back for all of you."
— Councillor Patricia Owens

Speaking at a town hall meeting, lead organiser David Hargreaves said the planning committee had worked tirelessly to secure funding and volunteer support. "We had over 200 volunteers sign up within the first 48 hours," he noted. "That tells you everything about how much this event means to people."

Tickets for ticketed sessions go on sale this Friday through the community portal website and at the borough library. Most activities, however, will remain free of charge and open to all visitors throughout the weekend. Families are encouraged to arrive early as parking will be limited near the main site.
